8. Design Analysis: Engineering and Proportions

The Wing Gundam stands approximately 13 cm at 1/144 scale, corresponding to an in-universe height of roughly 16.3 m. At that scale, a single armor part is on the order of 10–20 mm in its longest dimension, and panel-line widths are routinely 0.15–0.30 mm. Three engineering observations follow.

8.1 Wall Thickness Governs Both Stiffness and Moldability

Design Constraint

HIPS armor halves on the HG Wing Gundam are typically 0.8–1.5 mm thick. Below ≈0.6 mm the runner becomes prone to short shots (incomplete fill) during injection because the melt freezes at the cavity wall before reaching the far end. Above ≈2.0 mm the part suffers visible sink marks on the show-side surface from differential cooling. The 0.8–1.5 mm range is the simultaneous solution to "stiff enough to survive snap-fit insertion forces" and "thin enough to mold cleanly at HG cycle times."

8.2 Joint Location Is Constrained by Polycap Geometry

Because polycaps are molded as discrete LLDPE inserts, every articulating joint on the kit must be sized around the available polycap inventory. Inspection of the runner shows that the HG Wing Gundam reuses approximately six distinct polycap shapes for shoulders, elbows, hips, knees, ankles, and the wing-root pivot.

This re-use is itself a manufacturing constraint: it minimizes the number of distinct PE molds and standardizes joint resistance across the kit.

8.3 Color Blocking Maps onto Runner Identity

The five primary colors of the kit — white (large surface armor), blue (boots, chest detail), red (shield, accents), yellow (V-fin and trim), and black (joints, weapons) — are not chosen aesthetically; they are chosen because System Injection multi-color molding can fit at most four colors per multi-color machine, so the layout team must decompose the design onto runners that respect that constraint.

Notable Case

The Wing Gundam's yellow V-fin is one of the smallest and thinnest parts in the kit; its molding tolerance is the limiting case for the entire mold tool. Black HIPS runners commonly carry a higher load of carbon-black pigment (≈2 wt.%), slightly raising the modulus and lowering surface gloss compared with the white runner.
