Plastic Origin

Understanding the materials that form Gundam model kits

2. Origin and Materials

We sampled two runners from a Bandai HG 1/144 Wing Gundam kit and analyzed each by FTIR spectroscopy in attenuated-total-reflectance (ATR) mode against a commercial polymer reference library. The two runners differ visibly in mechanical character: SB-13 holds the rigid armor pieces; PC7 is the polycap runner of black, rubber-like joint inserts.

2.1 SB-13 Runner — High Impact Polystyrene (HIPS)

The spectral evidence is consistent with HIPS for three reasons. First, the aromatic C–H stretch near 3025 cm⁻¹ and the ring modes near 1600, 1490, and 1447 cm⁻¹ are diagnostic of polystyrene's mono-substituted benzene ring. Second, the strong methylene stretches at 2924 cm⁻¹ and 2846 cm⁻¹ indicate substantial sp³ carbon content from the polybutadiene rubber phase. Third, the close library match to "Polystyrene High Impact" rather than to general-purpose polystyrene is itself a categorical assignment by the spectrometer's nearest-neighbor algorithm.

HIPS is a rubber-modified thermoplastic in which 5–15 wt.% of polybutadiene is dispersed as roughly 1–3 μm domains throughout a rigid polystyrene continuous phase. The rubber phase absorbs and dissipates impact energy by initiating multiple silver-craze bands rather than allowing a single fast-propagating crack.

FTIR Top Matches — SB-13

Polystyrene High Impact (Butyl) · Bunatex (rubber-modified styrenic) · Polystyrene-ethylene-butylene block copolymer

2.2 PC7 Runner — Polyethylene-Based Polycap

The PC7 runner supplies the dark polycap parts that act as joint bushings in HG and most MG kits. Unlike the rigid SB-13 armor pieces, polycaps are designed to flex and provide a press-fit interference connection that retains over many cycles of pose changes.

The PC7 spectrum is unambiguous: four classic polyethylene fingerprints are present. The 2914 and 2847 cm⁻¹ doublet corresponds to asymmetric and symmetric stretching of the methylene backbone; 1462 cm⁻¹ is the CH₂ scissoring vibration; 720–730 cm⁻¹ is the rocking doublet diagnostic for long methylene sequences and crystallinity.

The pliability comes from polyethylene's intrinsic combination of high backbone flexibility and partial crystallinity (≈30–55% for LLDPE) — not from a rubber blend as initially hypothesized.

FTIR Top Matches — PC7

Polyethylene Hostalen GM 7040 (LyondellBasell) · Polyethylene linear low density · Paraffin (processing aid)

2.3 Why These Two Polymers?

Engineering Decision

The pairing is deliberate engineering, not cost minimization. HIPS supplies rigid armor with fine surface detail and paint receptivity. LLDPE supplies compliant polycap joints with elastic recovery and low surface energy for smooth re-posing. Pairing a stiff matrix with a soft elastomeric joint is the same strategy used in interference-fit medical-device hubs and automotive grommets — Gunpla applies it at toy scale.
